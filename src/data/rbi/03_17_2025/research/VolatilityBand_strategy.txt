STRATEGY_NAME: VolatilityBand

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
The VolatilityBand strategy combines a dynamic EMA (Exponential Moving Average) with Bollinger Bands to create a responsive trading system that adapts to market volatility. The strategy uses volatility to adjust the EMA lookback period and incorporates Bollinger Bands with color-coded signals for bullish and bearish conditions. Here's a detailed breakdown:

---

### **Key Strategy Components**
1. **Dynamic EMA**:  
   - The EMA's lookback period is adjusted based on market volatility. Higher volatility results in a shorter EMA period for faster responsiveness, while lower volatility uses a longer EMA period for smoother trends.
   - Volatility can be measured using ATR (Average True Range) or standard deviation.

2. **Bollinger Bands**:  
   - Bollinger Bands are colored green during bullish periods and red during bearish periods.  
   - The bands consist of a middle line (SMA or EMA), an upper band (middle line + 2 standard deviations), and a lower band (middle line - 2 standard deviations).  

3. **Signal Lines**:  
   - Two signal lines are used: one representing the dynamic EMA and the other representing the middle line of the Bollinger Bands.  
   - Crosses between these lines generate trade signals.

---

### **Entry Rules**
1. **Bullish Entry**:  
   - When the dynamic EMA crosses above the middle Bollinger Band line (green band), it signals a bullish trend.  
   - Confirm with price action staying above the lower Bollinger Band.  

2. **Bearish Entry**:  
   - When the dynamic EMA crosses below the middle Bollinger Band line (red band), it signals a bearish trend.  
   - Confirm with price action staying below the upper Bollinger Band.  

---

### **Exit Rules**
1. **Take Profit**:  
   - Exit when price reaches the opposite Bollinger Band (upper band for long trades, lower band for short trades).  

2. **Stop Loss**:  
   - Place a stop loss below the lower Bollinger Band for long trades or above the upper Bollinger Band for short trades.  

3. **Trend Reversal**:  
   - Exit if the dynamic EMA crosses back below the middle Bollinger Band line (for long trades) or above it (for short trades).  

---

### **Risk Management**
1. **Position Sizing**:  
   - Use a fixed percentage of capital (e.g., 1-2%) per trade to manage risk.  

2. **Volatility Filter**:  
   - Avoid trading during extremely high volatility (e.g., ATR above a certain threshold) to reduce risk.  

3. **Stop Loss Adjustment**:  
   - Adjust stop loss levels dynamically based on the width of the Bollinger Bands. Wider bands indicate higher volatility, so use wider stops.  

---

### **Required Indicators**
1. **Dynamic EMA**:  
   - Adjustable lookback period based on volatility.  

2. **Bollinger Bands**:  
   - Standard settings (20-period SMA with 2 standard deviations).  
   - Color-coded based on trend direction (green for bullish, red for bearish).  

3. **Volatility Measure**:  
   - ATR (Average True Range) or standard deviation to adjust the EMA lookback period.  

---

### **Summary**
The VolatilityBand strategy is a dynamic, volatility-adaptive system that uses a combination of a variable EMA and Bollinger Bands to identify high-probability trade setups. By adjusting the EMA period based on market conditions and using color-coded Bollinger Bands, the strategy provides clear entry and exit signals while managing risk effectively. This approach is particularly suited for trending markets with moderate volatility.
