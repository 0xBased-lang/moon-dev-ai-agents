STRATEGY_NAME: VolSpikeBreakout  

STRATEGY_DETAILS:  

**Key Strategy Components:**  
1. **Volatility Breakout:** Uses Bollinger Bands to identify volatility expansion.  
2. **Volume Confirmation:** Requires a volume spike (2x 20-day average) to validate the breakout.  
3. **Mean Reversion Exit:** Exits when price retraces to the 20-period moving average (MA).  

**Entry Rules:**  
- **Condition 1:** Price closes above the upper Bollinger Band (default 20-period, 2 standard deviations).  
- **Condition 2:** Volume exceeds 2x the 20-day average volume.  
- **Entry Action:** Enter long at the next candle's open.  

**Exit Rules:**  
- **Primary Exit:** Close the position when price touches or crosses below the 20-period MA.  
- **Optional Stop-Loss:** A fixed percentage (e.g., 2-3%) or a volatility-based stop (e.g., lower Bollinger Band) can be added for additional risk control.  

**Risk Management:**  
1. **Position Sizing:** Allocate a fixed percentage of capital per trade (e.g., 1-2%).  
2. **Stop-Loss:** Optional but recommended to limit downside (e.g., 2x the average true range (ATR) for dynamic stops).  
3. **Volume Filter:** Avoid false breakouts by requiring the 2x volume spike.  

**Required Indicators:**  
1. **Bollinger Bands** (20-period, 2 standard deviations).  
2. **20-Period Simple Moving Average (SMA)** for exit signal.  
3. **Volume Indicator** (20-day average volume for comparison).  
4. **Optional:** ATR for dynamic stop-loss calculation.  

**Unique Aspects:**  
- Combines volatility breakout with volume confirmation for higher-probability entries.  
- Exits on mean reversion to the 20 MA, capturing the "impulse-retracement" cycle.  
- Volume filter reduces false signals in low-liquidity environments.  

**Best Suited For:**  
- High-volatility assets (e.g., crypto, growth stocks).  
- Intraday to swing trading (timeframe-agnostic but works best on 1H-4H charts).  

**Notes:**  
- Backtest to optimize Bollinger Band settings (e.g., 14-period vs. 20-period).  
- Avoid using during low-volume periods (e.g., holidays).
