STRATEGY_NAME: DivergentCrossover

STRATEGY_DETAILS:
The DivergentCrossover strategy combines RSI divergence, MACD crossover signals, and the Commodity Channel Index (CCI) to identify high-probability trading opportunities. This strategy focuses on capturing momentum shifts while managing risk through trend strength confirmation.

### Key Strategy Components:
1. **RSI Divergence**: Identifies potential reversals by comparing price action with RSI momentum.
2. **MACD Crossover**: Confirms momentum shifts using the MACD line and signal line crossovers.
3. **MACD Histogram**: Provides additional insight into momentum strength and direction.
4. **CCI Trend Strength**: Acts as an exit filter to avoid trading against strong trends.

### Required Indicators:
1. **Relative Strength Index (RSI)**: Set to 14 periods.
2. **Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD)**: Default settings (12, 26, 9).
3. **Commodity Channel Index (CCI)**: Set to 20 periods.

---

### Entry Rules:
1. **Bullish Entry**:
   - RSI shows bullish divergence (price makes lower lows, RSI makes higher lows).
   - MACD line crosses above the signal line (bullish crossover).
   - MACD Histogram is positive and increasing.
   - CCI is below +100 (indicating no extreme overbought condition).

2. **Bearish Entry**:
   - RSI shows bearish divergence (price makes higher highs, RSI makes lower highs).
   - MACD line crosses below the signal line (bearish crossover).
   - MACD Histogram is negative and decreasing.
   - CCI is above -100 (indicating no extreme oversold condition).

---

### Exit Rules:
1. **Bullish Exit**:
   - CCI rises above +100, indicating strong bullish trend strength.
   - MACD Histogram starts decreasing after a peak.
   - RSI crosses below 70 (overbought condition).

2. **Bearish Exit**:
   - CCI falls below -100, indicating strong bearish trend strength.
   - MACD Histogram starts increasing after a trough.
   - RSI crosses above 30 (oversold condition).

---

### Risk Management:
1. **Stop Loss**:
   - Place stop loss below the recent swing low for long trades.
   - Place stop loss above the recent swing high for short trades.
   - Use a fixed percentage (e.g., 1-2% of account balance) or ATR-based stop loss.

2. **Position Sizing**:
   - Risk no more than 1-2% of the account balance per trade.
   - Adjust position size based on stop loss distance.

3. **Take Profit**:
   - Use a risk-reward ratio of at least 1:2.
   - Consider trailing stops to lock in profits during strong trends.

---

### Additional Notes:
- **Timeframe**: Best suited for 1-hour (H1) or 4-hour (H4) charts.
- **Market Conditions**: Works well in ranging or mildly trending markets. Avoid trading during extreme volatility or news events.
- **Confirmation**: Wait for candle closes to confirm signals and avoid false entries.

This strategy combines divergence, momentum, and trend strength to create a robust trading system. Always backtest and optimize parameters for your specific trading instrument and market conditions.
